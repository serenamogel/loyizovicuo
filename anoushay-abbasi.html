Anoushay Abbasi is a Pakistani former VJ, an actress, and a model. She has worked on several television serial as a supporting actress. The actress was born on 24th November 1983, in Lahore, Pakistan. She belongs to an artistic family. She has two siblings, a sister, and a brother. Her sister is an actress, Javeria Abbasi, and her brother is an actor Shamoon Abbasi. She got married to an actor who is the son of a cricketer, Taslim Arif and actress, Rubina Arif on 27th September 2014 named Ainan Arif,

Career Journey

Anoushay Abbasi began her career journey in the show business as a Video Jockey at various television channels, such as MTV Pakistan, Aag TV, Geo TV, etc. After her success in this field, she started her acting career and made her acting debut in a Hum TV drama serial “Mere Aangne Main” in 2010 which was her first drama after that drama she also portrayed in many drama serials. Her famous drama included “Mera Saaein 2” which is a 2012 Pakistani Political drama where she plays the role of home mate Narjis. This drama was aired on ARY Digital and drama main casts are Fahad Mustafa, Aiza Khan Aamina Sheikh and Mahnoor Baloch.

A Pakistani Geo TV telenovela “Tootay Huwae Per” where she played the lead role opposite Ayesha khan in 2013. This drama is the story of the inspiration and dreams of a middle-class girl. Anoushay has also appeared in the ARY Digital horror drama serial “Kaala Jadu” opposite Waseem Abbas.

Her most successful dramas are “Roshini Andhera Roshini” on ATV for which she garnered the Lux Style Award. She also appeared in the famous drama serial of Geo TV drama “Malika e Aliya” where she played the role of Chanda with Maria Wasti. This drama is the story of evil plans of a selfish girl Alia to become the owner of her in-laws’ home. Nex then, she also appeared the same role in the sequel of this drama named “Malika e Aliya 2”.

Her last drama was the romantic serial “Yeh Mera Deewanapan Hai” along with Saima Noor, and Javed Sheikh on the ATV channel in 2017. Till today, she has not appeared in any drama.
