Biography

Michael Martin Murphey was born in Oak Cliff, TX on March 14, 1945. Known for his Gold-designated album Cowboy Songs and for his hit singles “Don’t Count the Rainy Days,” “What’s Forever For,” and “Wildfire,” Murphey is also famous for writing the New Mexico state song “The Land of Enchantment.” Country music legend Kenny Rogers recorded an album titled The Ballad of Calico that consists entirely of songs written by Murphey. While working toward an undergraduate degree in Greek at the University of North Texas, he became a member of the college’s Folk Music Club. He later attended the University of California, Los Angeles, where he studied Medieval history and creative writing and continued writing and performing music.
