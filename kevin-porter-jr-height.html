Kevin Porter Jr.’s career

Porter was chosen by the Milwaukee Bucks as the 30th overall pick in the 2019 NBA Draft; however, he was eventually transferred to the Cleveland Cavaliers via the Detroit Pistons.

The Cleveland Cavaliers made the announcement that they had signed Porter on July 3, 2019. On October 23, Porter made his NBA debut. He took part in the Orlando Magic’s 85-94 loss and finished the game with one rebound, two assists, and a steal.

Porter was penalized on November 4 for unlawfully making contact with a referee and was benched for one game without pay.

In a 123-105 loss against the New York Knicks on November 19, his first NBA start for the Cavaliers, he scored a then-career-high 18 points in 31 minutes.

Porter was sidelined to begin the 2020–21 season because of an offseason firearms charge that was ultimately dropped.

Following an outburst involving a locker change after the Cavaliers acquired Taurean Prince from the Brooklyn Nets on January 18, 2021, the Cavaliers declared that Porter would either be traded or terminated. Three days later, having not appeared in a single game for the Cavaliers throughout the 2020–21 season, he was dealt to the Rockets.

Porter was dealt to the Houston Rockets on January 21, 2021, in exchange for a future top-55 protected second-round pick. Later, he was transferred to the Rio Grande Valley Vipers, the Rockets’ G League club, where he made his professional debut on February 10, 2021.

He achieved the first triple-double of the G League campaign on February 25 by scoring 27 points, grabbing 11 rebounds, and distributing 14 assists. After an outstanding showing in the G-League on March 6, the Rockets formally recalled Porter from the Vipers.

On March 11, 2021, Porter made his formal Rockets debut, contributing 13 points, 10 assists, and five rebounds in a defeat to the Sacramento Kings.

Porter received a $50,000 punishment on April 28 for breaking the league’s health and safety policies. He became the youngest player in NBA history to have 50+ points and 10+ assists in a game the day after the incident, defeating the Milwaukee Bucks with 50 points and 11 assists.

Porter only participated in 26 games with the Rockets throughout the regular season, despite the numerous issues he was facing. He averaged 16.6 points per game, 4.4 percent of the team’s rebounds, 6.3 assists, and 3.1 turnovers.

Porter and teammate Christian Wood got into a verbal argument with Rockets assistant coach John Lucas during halftime of a 111-124 loss to the Denver Nuggets on January 1, 2022.

Porter then hurled something into the locker room before leaving the Toyota Center, where the Rockets were playing.

Porter and Wood were both given one-game suspensions for their actions, according to Rockets head coach Stephen Silas, who made the announcement on January 3.

In a 121-118 loss to Sacramento on March 31, 2022, he earned his first triple-double with 30 points, 12 rebounds, and 12 assists.

Porter and the Rockets reached a four-year, $82.5 million contract deal on October 17, 2022. In a 130-125 loss against Memphis on March 23, 2023, he earned his second triple-double with 14 points, 10 rebounds, and 10 assists.

Porter was traded to the Oklahoma City Thunder on October 17, 2023, as a result of his domestic assault accusation.
